Conclusions of this Project¶

Note I did not stratify the dataset as nearly 80% of all commands were 0 and 2. Both CNNs (trained on 0.9x0.8~75% or 22.5k of the 31.4k images captured) failed to generalise well; achieving only 47% and 48% CA on test images. The 1st CNN is disregarded; failing to even learn the training examples (51% compared with 93% for the other). However, they both attained poor rewards and only made the correct action 30% of occasions (as advised by heuristic). The MLP (trained on 0.8x0.8 ~ 64% or 161k of the total 252k states captured), generalised extremely well, and the decision tree and KNN (k=5) likewise, with test CAs of 99%, 87% and 85%. All three obtained great rewards, but the MLP was by far the best algorithm making the heuristic-advised move 98.6% of the time. Perhaps the disparity between the image and state models is that the state models were trained on 5 times as many examples, and could generalise much better as high-level representations made it easier to draw connections between similar events due to capturing universal information about position of the Lundar-Lander in only 8 numbers (vs 1x64x64=4096). Moreover I didn't stop the CNNs when their training and validation accuracies diverged, which worsened generalisation. The state models were also (66secs_per_epoch/3secs_per_epoch) 22 times quicker to train. The DQN agent was trained on 2mil examples (or steps) which took much longer than the other models (6.8 and 50 times more than the Atari CNN and MLP respectively). Its rewards were reasonably high. Since we never run out of training examples the DQN could challenge the MLP if it played long enough, so long as the agent keeps improving its policy (which is thusfar the case see the mean reward per step chart). However, the MLP is considered to have already "solved" the Lunar Lander problem by averaging over 200pts per episode. Thus, the lesson may be that with the right high-level representations in a small state-space problem, classifiers on expert moves can perform accurately and more efficiently than RL algorithms. However, usually most of those conditions are not met.
